Global Human Resources Policy
Version 3.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Document ID: HR-POL-2025-001
Section 1: Code of Conduct
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct, integrity, and ethics in all business dealings. This includes treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and dignity.
Section 2: Compensation & Benefits
Section 2.1: Salary Structure
TechWave Inc. maintains competitive salary structures aligned with industry standards. Annual salary reviews are conducted in Q4 of each fiscal year.
Section 2.2: Performance Bonuses
Quarterly performance bonuses are awarded based on individual and team achievements. Eligibility criteria and bonus amounts are determined by department leadership in consultation with HR.
Section 3: Time Off & Leave
Section 3.1: Annual Leave
All full-time employees receive a minimum of 25 days annual leave per year, increasing with tenure. Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave entitlements.
Section 3.2: Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to paid sick leave as per local labor laws. Medical certificates may be required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.
Section 4: Travel & Expenses
Section 4.5: Business Travel Policy
Section 4.5.1: General Travel Guidelines
All business travel must be pre-approved by direct managers. Employees should book travel through SAP Concur to ensure compliance with company policies and to streamline expense reporting.
Section 4.5.2: Flight and Accommodation Standards
Economy class flights are standard for trips under 6 hours. Premium economy or business class may be approved for flights exceeding 6 hours or when medically necessary. Hotel accommodations should be reasonable and within the company's negotiated rates where available.
Section 4.5.3: Per Diem Allowances for High-Cost Cities
Per Diem Allowances for High-Cost Metropolitan Areas
TechWave Inc. follows the U.S. General Services Administration (GSA) guidelines for per diem rates in high-cost metropolitan areas. These rates cover meals and incidental expenses (M&IE) during business travel.
The following cities are classified as high-cost and receive enhanced per diem rates:
City Daily Per Diem Rate Breakdown (Meals + Incidentals)
San Francisco, CA $79 Meals: $64 | Incidentals: $15
New York, NY $79 Meals: $64 | Incidentals: $15
Atlanta, GA $89 Meals: $74 | Incidentals: $15
Seattle, WA $79 Meals: $64 | Incidentals: $15
Boston, MA $79 Meals: $64 | Incidentals: $15
Los Angeles, CA $74 Meals: $59 | Incidentals: $15
Chicago, IL $79 Meals: $64 | Incidentals: $15
Important Notes:
• Per diem rates are automatically calculated based on travel destination and duration in SAP Concur
• Employees do not need to submit individual meal receipts when claiming per diem allowances
• Per diem covers breakfast, lunch, dinner, and incidental expenses such as tips and service charges
• First and last day of travel receive 75% of the daily per diem rate
• If meals are provided as part of conferences or client meetings, per diem should be adjusted accordingly
• For extended stays (14+ days), consult with HR for adjusted rates
Section 4.5.4: Expense Reporting
All travel expenses must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion through SAP Concur. Late submissions may result in delayed reimbursement or non-payment of expenses. Ensure all required documentation is attached to your expense report.
Section 5: Professional Development
TechWave Inc. encourages continuous learning and development. Employees may request funding for professional courses, certifications, and conferences relevant to their role with manager approval.
Section 6: Remote Work Policy
Hybrid and remote work arrangements are available based on role requirements and manager discretion. All remote work must be formalized through HR with appropriate documentation.
